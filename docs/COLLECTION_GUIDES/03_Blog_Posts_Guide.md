# Blog Posts Collection Guide

## What is the Posts Collection?

The **Posts** collection manages your blog articles. Use it to create educational content, share news, improve SEO, and engage with visitors.

## Common Tasks

### Creating a New Blog Post

1. Go to **Posts** in the left sidebar
2. Click **Create Item** (or the "+" button)
3. Fill in the required fields (see Field Guide below)
4. Write your article content
5. Upload a featured image
6. Set **Status** to "Draft" while writing
7. Click **Save**
8. Review your post
9. Change **Status** to "Published" when ready
10. Click **Save**

### Editing an Existing Post

1. Go to **Posts**
2. Find the post you want to edit (use search or filter)
3. Click on the post title
4. Make your changes
5. Click **Save**

### Publishing a Post

1. Edit your post
2. Ensure all content is complete
3. Set **Status** to "Published"
4. Set **Published Date** (or leave for current date)
5. Click **Save**

## Field-by-Field Guide

### Post Title
- **What it is:** The headline of your blog post
- **Where it appears:** Blog listings, article header, search results
- **Best practice:** Make it engaging and descriptive
- **Length:** 50-70 characters ideal
- **Examples:**
  - "5 Tips for Maintaining Healthy Teeth"
  - "Everything You Need to Know About Dental Implants"
  - "How to Choose the Right Dentist"

### URL Slug
- **What it is:** The URL part for the blog post
- **Format:** Lowercase, use hyphens, no spaces
- **Examples:**
  - "5-tips-healthy-teeth" → yoursite.com/blog/5-tips-healthy-teeth
  - "dental-implants-guide" → yoursite.com/blog/dental-implants-guide
- **Tip:** Leave blank to auto-generate from title

### Article Content
- **What it is:** The main blog post content
- **Format:** Rich text editor (supports formatting, headings, lists, links)
- **Best practice:**
  - Use headings to organize content (H2, H3)
  - Break up text with paragraphs
  - Use bullet points and lists
  - Include relevant images within content
  - Write naturally, include keywords

### Excerpt
- **What it is:** Short summary of the post
- **Where it appears:** Blog listings, previews, social sharing
- **Length:** 1-2 sentences (100-150 characters)
- **Purpose:** Tease the content and encourage clicks
- **Tip:** If left empty, first paragraph of content will be used
- **Example:** "Learn five simple tips to keep your teeth healthy and avoid common dental problems."

### Featured Image
- **What it is:** Main image for the blog post
- **Where it appears:** Blog listings, top of article, social sharing
- **Recommended size:** 1200x630px (optimal for social media)
- **Format:** JPG or PNG
- **Tip:** Use engaging, relevant images that represent the content

### Author
- **What it is:** Who wrote the post
- **Purpose:** Attribution and credibility
- **How to use:** Select from dropdown (usually yourself or team members)
- **Tip:** Ensure authors are set up in the system first

### Status
- **Draft:** Post not visible, still being written
- **Published:** Post is live on website
- **Archived:** Post is hidden but kept

### Published Date
- **What it is:** When the post was/will be published
- **Purpose:** Controls sorting and display order
- **Tip:** Leave empty to use current date when publishing
- **Use case:** Schedule posts for future publication

### SEO Title
- **What it is:** Title for search engines
- **Length:** 50-60 characters
- **Tip:** Include main keyword, make it compelling
- **Example:** "5 Tips for Healthy Teeth | Dental Care Guide"

### SEO Description
- **What it is:** Description for search results
- **Length:** 150-160 characters
- **Tip:** Write a compelling summary that encourages clicks
- **Example:** "Discover five essential tips for maintaining healthy teeth. Expert advice from dental professionals. Read more!"

## Best Practices

### Writing Blog Posts

1. **Start with a hook** - Grab attention in the first paragraph
2. **Use headings** - Organize content with H2, H3 headings
3. **Keep paragraphs short** - 2-4 sentences per paragraph
4. **Use lists** - Bullet points and numbered lists are easy to scan
5. **Include images** - Break up text with relevant images
6. **Write naturally** - Use conversational tone
7. **Include keywords** - But don't stuff them unnaturally
8. **End with action** - Call to action or summary

### Content Ideas

- How-to guides
- Tips and advice
- Industry news
- Patient stories (with permission)
- Service explanations
- FAQ answers
- Health information
- Behind-the-scenes content

### SEO Tips

1. **Research keywords** - What are people searching for?
2. **Use keywords naturally** - In title, headings, and content
3. **Write for humans first** - Then optimize for search engines
4. **Internal linking** - Link to related services and pages
5. **Regular posting** - Fresh content helps SEO
6. **Complete SEO fields** - Title and description are important

### Image Guidelines

1. **Use high-quality images** - Professional, clear photos
2. **Optimize file size** - Keep under 500KB when possible
3. **Use descriptive filenames** - Helps with SEO
4. **Add alt text** - Describe images for accessibility
5. **Featured image** - Make it engaging and shareable

## Examples

### Example: Creating a "Dental Care Tips" Post

1. Go to **Posts** → Create Item
2. **Post Title:** "10 Essential Tips for Daily Dental Care"
3. **Slug:** "10-essential-dental-care-tips" (auto-generates)
4. **Status:** Draft
5. **Article Content:**
   ```
   Introduction paragraph about importance of dental care...
   
   ## Tip 1: Brush Twice Daily
   Explanation...
   
   ## Tip 2: Use Proper Technique
   Explanation...
   
   [Continue with all 10 tips]
   
   ## Conclusion
   Summary and call to action...
   ```
6. **Excerpt:** "Discover 10 essential tips for maintaining healthy teeth and gums. Simple daily habits that make a big difference."
7. **Featured Image:** Upload image of healthy teeth or dental care
8. **Author:** Select yourself
9. **Published Date:** Leave empty (will use current date)
10. **SEO Title:** "10 Essential Dental Care Tips | Expert Advice"
11. **SEO Description:** "Learn 10 essential tips for daily dental care. Expert advice to keep your teeth healthy and prevent problems."
12. Save as Draft
13. Review and edit
14. Change to Published
15. Save

### Example: Editing a Published Post

1. Go to **Posts**
2. Find the post
3. Click to edit
4. Make corrections or updates
5. Save (changes appear immediately if published)

## Publishing Workflow

### Recommended Process

1. **Create as Draft** - Start writing
2. **Fill all fields** - Title, content, image, SEO
3. **Review carefully** - Check spelling, grammar, facts
4. **Set Published Date** - Current or future date
5. **Change to Published** - Make it live
6. **Save** - Post is now visible

### Scheduling Posts

To schedule a post for future publication:
1. Create post as Draft
2. Set **Published Date** to future date
3. Set **Status** to "Published"
4. Save
5. Post will appear on the scheduled date

## Troubleshooting

**Q: My post isn't showing on the website**
- Check status is "Published"
- Verify published date is not in the future
- Make sure you clicked Save

**Q: The excerpt is too long**
- Edit the excerpt field
- Keep it to 1-2 sentences
- If empty, first paragraph of content is used

**Q: Images aren't displaying**
- Go to **Files** and verify image uploaded
- Re-select the featured image
- Check image format (JPG, PNG)

**Q: I want to unpublish a post**
- Edit the post
- Change **Status** from "Published" to "Draft" or "Archived"
- Save

**Q: Can I edit a published post?**
- Yes! Edit and save
- Changes appear immediately on the website
- Be careful with published content
